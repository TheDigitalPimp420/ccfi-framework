# Cognitive Control-Flow Interruption (CCFI)

**A control-flow metaphor for belief updating and learning-by-contradiction**

**Author:** Jesse James Leighty  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** January 10, 2026  

---

## Abstract

Cognitive Control-Flow Interruption (CCFI) is a communication and teaching framework
that explains why new information often fails to change entrenched beliefs.
Rather than treating belief change as a problem of persuasion or information delivery,
CCFI models reasoning as control flow: once a belief loop is validated, new information
is repeatedly routed back to the same conclusion.

CCFI proposes that learning occurs when a contradiction interrupts the active reasoning
loop itself, creating a pause where the existing model cannot resolve the situation.
This pause enables self-directed belief updating rather than compliance-driven persuasion.

CCFI aligns with established research on prediction error, cognitive conflict, and
conceptual change, while offering a practical, operational metaphor for educators,
writers, journalists, and communicators.

---

## The Problem: Why Information Often Fails

In many contexts—education, journalism, public discourse—adding more information
does not lead to learning or belief change.

This is not because people are unintelligent or resistant by nature.
It is because reasoning tends to reuse validated interpretive pathways.
New information that fits the pathway reinforces the conclusion.
Information that does not fit is often dismissed before it is meaningfully processed.

As a result, facts alone rarely change minds.

---

## The CCFI Model

CCFI models belief maintenance as control flow.

A simplified representation:

10 IF belief_validated THEN GOTO 10
20 IF contradiction_detected THEN GOTO 10

The loop is stable and efficient.

Information added outside the loop is never reached:

30 PRINT "New information"

CCFI introduces a conditional inside the loop:

15 IF inconsistency_unresolved THEN GOTO 40

This creates an execution pause.
The system cannot proceed without reevaluating its assumptions.

Learning occurs at this interruption point.

---

## How Learning Occurs

When a contradiction cannot be resolved by the existing model:

- Automatic reasoning pauses
- Defensiveness decreases
- Cognitive flexibility increases
- New explanations become reachable

Crucially, the learner updates their model voluntarily,
because the old path no longer works.

---

## Applications

### Education
Designing lessons where students encounter cases their existing models cannot explain,
prompting discovery rather than correction.

### Writing and Journalism
Structuring narratives that guide readers into familiar reasoning
before revealing internal inconsistencies and offering new frameworks.

### Media Literacy
Helping individuals recognize belief loops and understand
how repetition reinforces conclusions.

### Conflict Reduction
Reducing identity threat by focusing on unresolved contradictions
rather than direct opposition.

---

## Ethical Design

CCFI is intended for ethical use.

It supports:
- Learning
- Clarity
- Understanding
- Intellectual autonomy

It does not support:
- Manipulation
- Coercion
- Entrapment
- Deception

The goal is insight, not compliance.

---

## Relationship to Existing Research

CCFI aligns with established work in:
- Predictive processing and prediction error
- Cognitive dissonance
- Schema violation
- Conceptual change in education

Its contribution is not a new neurological claim,
but a practical operational framing that explains
why information is often unreachable until a belief loop is interrupted.

---

## Conclusion

Beliefs do not change because information is added.
They change because an existing path stops working.

Cognitive Control-Flow Interruption provides a framework
for understanding and designing that moment.
